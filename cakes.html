Classic Sponge Cake

Ingredients

  • 200g soft butter, plus extra for greasing
  • 200g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 200g golden caster sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 tbsp milk

    • For the filling
  • 142ml double cream
  • 50g golden caster sugar
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 100g strawberry conserve
  • icing sugar, for dusting

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/gas. Grease and base-line 2 x 20cm non-stick round sandwich tins, line with greased parchment.
  2. Mix the flour and baking powder into a large bowl, add in all the other ingredients for the cake mixture.
  3. Whisk everything until smooth, an electric whisk is advised.
  4. Divide the mix between the cake tins, bake for 20-25 minutes until cooked and golden.
  5. When cool enough, remove from tins and allow for further cooling.
To Make the Filling
  1. Whip the cream with the caster sugar and vanilla until it holds its shape.
  2. Build the cake by spreaking one sponge with jam and the other with cream.
  3. Sandwich the whole thing together, then dust with icing sugar.

Chocolate and Banana Cake

Ingredients

  • 100ml sunflower oil, plus extra to grease
  • 175g self-raising flour
  • 175g caster sugar
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 4 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 100g chocolate chips or chuncks
  • 175g very ripe bananas
  • 3 medium eggs, 2 seperated
  • 50ml milk

    • For the filling
  • 100g milk chocolate
  • 100ml soured cream
  • handful of dried banana chips, roughly chopped


Method

  1. Preheat oven to 160 C / Gas 3. Grease and line a 2lb loaf tin with baking parchment, allow it to come 2cm above the top of the tin.
  2. Mix the sugar, flour, bicarbonate, cocoa and chocolate in a large bowl.
  3. Mash the bananas in a bowl and stir in the whole egg plus 2 yolks, followed by the oil and milk.
  4. Beat the egg whites until stiff. Quickly stir the wet banana mixture into the dry ingredients, stir in a quarter of the egg whites to loosen the mixture, then gently fold in the rest.
  5. Gently scrape into the tin and bake for 1 hr 10-15 mins, or until a skewer inserted comes out clean. Then leave to cool on a rack.
To Make the Filling
  1. Melt the chocolate and soured cream together in a heatproof bowl over a pan of barely simmering water.
  2. Chill in the fridge until spreadable. Remove cake from tin, roughly swirl icing over and scatter with the banana chips.

Cappuccino Cake

Ingredients

  • 250g pack butter, softened
  • 250g light soft brown sugar plus 2-3 tbsp
  • 300g self-raising flour
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 50g walnuts, toasted and finely chopped (a food processor is easiest), optional
  • 200ml very strong coffee (made fresh or with instant), cooled

    • For the frosting
  • 500g tub mascarpone
  • 2 tbsp light soft brown sugar
  • cocoa powder or drinking chocolate to decorate

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 180 C / Gas 4. Butter 2 x 20cm sandwich tins and line the bottoms with greaseproof paper..
  2. Beat butter and sugar together with electric beaters until pale and creamy. Add the flour and eggs in one go and keep beating until evenly mixed.
  3. Fold in the walnuts (if using) and half of the coffee.
  4. Spoon the mix into the prepared tins and bake for 25-30 mins or until golden and well risen.
  5. Leave the cakes in their tins for 5 mins before turning onto a wire rack. Sweeten the remaining coffee with the extra sugar and sprinkle 4 tbsp over the sponges. Leave to cool completely.
To Make the Filling
  1. Tip the mascarpone into a large bowl and beat in the sugar and remaining coffee until smooth and creamy.
  2. Use about half of the frosting to sandwich the sponges together then, using a palette or cutlery knife, spread the rest of the frosting over the top of the cake.
  3. Decorate with a dusting of cocoa powder or drinking chocolate.
