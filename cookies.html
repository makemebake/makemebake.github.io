Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients

Makes approx. 14
  • 150 grams soft unsalted butter
  • 125 grams soft light brown sugar
  • 100 grams caster sugar
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 egg (fridge-cold)
  • 1 egg yolk (fridge-cold)
  • 300 grams flour
  • ½ teaspoon bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 x 326 grams packet milk chocolate chips

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 170°C/325°F. Line a baking sheet with baking parchment
  2. Melt the butter and let it cool a bit. Put the brown and white sugars into a bowl, pour the slightly cooled, melted butter over them and beat together
  3. Beat in the vanilla, the cold egg and cold egg yolk until your mixture is light and creamy
  4. Slowly mix in the flour and bicarb until just blended, then fold in the chocolate chips
  5. Scoop the cookie dough into an American quarter-cup measure or a 60ml/quarter cup round icecream scoop and drop onto the prepared baking sheet, plopping the cookies down about 8cm/3 inches apart. You will need to make these in 2 batches, keeping the bowl of cookie dough in the fridge between batches
  6. Bake for 15–17 minutes in the preheated oven, or until the edges are lightly toasted. Cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to wire racks

Peanut Butter Cookies

Ingredients

Makes approx. 6
  • 260g Peanut Butter
  • 200g Caster Sugar
  • 1 Egg
  • A handful of peanuts to sprinkle on top of the cookies


Method

This is a really simple recipe that will have you baking inno time!
  1. Preheat oven to 180 C / Gas 4. Line baking trays with baking parchment.
  2. Combine the peanut butter, sugar and egg. Mix until smooth.
  3. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking tray.
  4. Sprinkle the tops of the cookies with peanuts.
  5. Bake cookies in preheated oven for 6 to 8 minutes.
    Try not to overbake - these cookies are best when they are still soft and barely brown on the bottom.
  6. Let cookies cool for 5 minutes before serving.

Oatmeal and Raisin Cookies

Ingredients

Makes approx. 12
  • 75g block butter
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 175g dark brown soft sugar
  • 200g medium oatmeal
  • 110g wholemeal flour
  • ½ teaspoon bicarbonate of soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 150g raisins to include within the cookies

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 180 C / Gas 4.
  2. Heat the butter in a saucepan very gently, once heated, leave to cool.
  3. Stir in the beaten egg to the now cooled melted butter.
  4. Put all other ingredigents into a bowl and add in the egg and butter mixture. Stir until the mixture becomes a stiff paste.
  5. Form the paste into small balls, and place them on greased basking sheets, make sure they have enough room to spread whilst baking.
  6. Press the down slightly.
  7. Bake in the centre of the oven for 16-18 minutes. Leave them to cool for a little and store them in an airtight container.
